# Nerviq License FAQ

Nerviq is licensed under the **MIT License** (relicensed from AGPL-3.0 in
v1.31.0, July 2026).

This FAQ answers common questions.

---

## What can I do freely?

Essentially everything:

**Use the CLI anywhere** — locally, in CI/CD, in your company, in client
projects. No restrictions, no obligations beyond keeping the MIT notice.

**Fork and modify** — fork the repo, modify it, keep your changes private or
publish them. Your choice.

**Embed and redistribute** — bundle Nerviq into proprietary products,
closed-source tools, or commercial SaaS platforms.

**Host as a service** — offer Nerviq functionality to third parties over a
network. Unlike the old AGPL terms, MIT imposes no source-sharing requirement
for network use.

**Use the output** — scores, reports, JSON, patches are your data.

## What must I do?

Keep the copyright and permission notice (the contents of `LICENSE`) in
copies or substantial portions of the Software. That's the entire obligation.

## What about the SDK and MCP server?

They ship inside `@nerviq/cli` and are covered by the same MIT license.

## I adopted Nerviq under AGPL-3.0. What changes for me?

Nothing breaks: versions up to and including v1.30.0 remain available under
AGPL-3.0 (a license grant is not revocable retroactively). From v1.31.0
onward, all releases are MIT. If AGPL was blocking your adoption (e.g. an
enterprise default-deny list), upgrade to v1.31.0+.

## Do I ever need a commercial license?

No. There is no commercial license requirement for any usage pattern. Paid
audits, support, and hosted offerings may be sold separately as services —
contact **business@nerviq.net**.
